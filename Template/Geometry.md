## 1. Fundamental Triangle Theorems

- **Exterior Angle Theorem:** The exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the two opposite interior angles.
$$\text{Exterior Angle} = \text{Interior}_1 + \text{Interior}_2$$

- **Triangle Inequality Theorem:** The sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be strictly greater than the length of the third side.
  $$a + b > c$$

## 2. Classification by Angles

|**Classification**|**Description**|
|---|---|
|**Acute**|All 3 angles are strictly less than 90°.|
|**Right**|Exactly one angle is exactly 90°.|
|**Obtuse**|Exactly one angle is strictly greater than 90°.|

## 3. The Heavyweight Triangle Laws (Trigonometry)

> **Note:** For these laws, standard notation dictates that angles are denoted by uppercase letters ($A, B, C$) and the sides opposite those angles are denoted by the corresponding lowercase letters ($a, b, c$).

### Right-Angled Triangles Only

- **Pythagorean Theorem:** Where $c$ is the hypotenuse.
$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

- **SOH CAH TOA:**
    - $\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$
    - $\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$
    - $\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}$
### Any Triangle (General Laws)

- **The Sine Rule:** Use this when you know a side and its opposite angle, plus one other side or angle.
 $$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$$
- **The Cosine Rule:** Use this when you know all three sides (SSS) or two sides and the included angle (SAS).

  $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C$$

  _(To find an angle, rearrange to: $\cos C = \frac{a^2 + b^2 - c^2}{2ab}$)
### Area of a Triangle

- **Standard Formula:** When you have the perpendicular height.
 $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot b \cdot h$$
- **Sine Area Formula:** When you have two sides and the included angle (SAS).
  $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ab \sin C$$
